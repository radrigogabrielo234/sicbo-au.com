Decoding the Super Sic Bo Table: Understanding Bets, Odds, and Multiplied Payouts

The Super Sic Bo table, while visually engaging with its multiplier highlights, is still based on the familiar Sic Bo layout. You’re betting on the various outcomes of three dice. Let’s revisit the common betting options and then see how those fantastic multipliers play into the payouts.

  • Small/Big Bets: Your safest bets, offering near even money.

    • Small: Sum of dice 4-10 (excluding triples).

    • Big: Sum of dice 11-17 (excluding triples).

    • Payout: 1:1. These bets have the lowest house edge, making them a solid foundation for your strategy.

  • Total Sum Bets: Bet on the exact sum of the three dice (from 4 to 17). Payout rates vary significantly based on rarity.

    • Example Payouts (pre-multiplier): Sum of 4 or 17 (60:1), Sum of 5 or 16 (30:1), Sum of 9, 10, 11, 12 (6:1).

  • Single Dice Bets: Bet a specific number (1-6) appears on one, two, or all three dice.

    • One die: 1:1; Two dice: 2:1; Three dice: 3:1.

  • Double Bets: Bet two specific numbers appear (e.g., two 4s).

    • Payout: Typically 8:1 or 10:1.

  • Triple Bets: Bet a specific number appears on all three dice (e.g., three 6s). This is where the big win potential truly explodes with multipliers!

    • Payout: Often 150:1 or 180:1 (before multipliers).

  • Any Triple Bet: Bet any triple appears.

    • Payout: Typically 24:1 or 30:1.

  • Combination Bets: Bet on a specific two-dice combination (e.g., 1 and 2).

    • Payout: 5:1.

The genius of Super Sic Bo is that any of these betting options (except for Small/Big in some versions, or sometimes the “Any Triple” bet, depending on the specific game rules) can randomly receive a multiplier.
