Why Responsible Gaming is Important

Signs of Gambling Problems

It’s important to recognize when gambling may be becoming a problem. Here are some common signs:

  • Spending more money and time on gambling than intended.

  • Chasing losses by gambling more in an attempt to win back money.

  • Gambling to escape stress, anxiety, or depression.

  • Feeling anxious, guilty, or upset about your gambling behavior.

  • Lying to friends or family about the extent of your gambling.

  • Neglecting important aspects of life, such as work or relationships, due to gambling.

If you notice these signs in yourself or others, it may be time to reassess your gambling habits.

Safe Gambling Recommendations

To ensure that your gambling remains enjoyable and within control, we recommend the following tips:

  • Set a budget and stick to it: Decide in advance how much money you are willing to spend, and never exceed that amount.

  • Limit your gambling time: Set a specific time limit for your gaming sessions to avoid prolonged play.

  • Never gamble under the influence: Avoid gambling when you’re stressed, angry, or under the influence of alcohol or other substances.

  • Take regular breaks: Step away from the game to refresh yourself, keeping your mind clear and focused.

  • Play for fun: Treat gambling as a form of entertainment, not as a way to make money.

By following these recommendations, you can maintain a healthy balance between fun and responsibility.
